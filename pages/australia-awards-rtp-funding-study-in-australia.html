Australia Awards & RTP: Funding Your Studies in Australia

🗓️ Jun 5, 2026⏱️ 12 min read✍️ AbroadReady Team

When people research study in Australia scholarships, two names come up again and again: the Australia Awards and the Research Training Program. Together they represent the backbone of government-funded study in Australia, one focused on development and one on research. Understanding how each works, and who they are for, is the fastest way to find funding that fits your situation.

Australia combines globally ranked universities, a high standard of living, and strong post-study work options. The best study in Australia scholarships can remove the biggest barrier, cost, by covering tuition and living expenses so you can focus on your degree.

Key takeaways

  • Australia Awards Scholarships are development-focused awards for students from eligible partner countries.
  • The Research Training Program (RTP) funds research master's and PhD students, covering tuition and often a living stipend.
  • RTP funding is distributed by universities, so you apply through the institution, not a central portal.
  • Australia Awards cover full costs including travel, tuition, and living allowances, plus academic support.
  • Most awards require you to secure admission to a course before or alongside your scholarship application.

Australia Awards Scholarships

The Australia Awards are long-term development scholarships funded by the Australian government, primarily through its foreign affairs and trade department. They aim to build skills and leadership in partner countries across the Indo-Pacific, Africa, and beyond, so eligibility is tied to a defined list of participating countries.

What they cover

Australia Awards Scholarships are genuinely comprehensive. They typically cover full tuition, return air travel, a one-time establishment allowance, and a contribution to living expenses. They also include Overseas Student Health Cover and introductory academic programs to help you settle into study. In some cases, additional academic or fieldwork support is available.

Who they are for

These awards target applicants who will use their qualification to contribute to the development of their home country. Applicants generally need to meet the eligibility criteria for their specific country, demonstrate leadership potential, and commit to returning home for a set period after finishing their studies. Selection considers academic record, professional and personal qualities, and development impact. If you are from an eligible country and motivated by public or community impact, this is one of the strongest study in Australia scholarships to pursue.

The Research Training Program (RTP)

The Research Training Program is the Australian government's main mechanism for funding research degrees. It supports both domestic and international students undertaking a research master's or a PhD at Australian universities. Rather than being awarded centrally, RTP funding is allocated to universities, which then distribute it to students through their own competitive processes.

What RTP offers

RTP support comes in a few forms. It can cover tuition fees for your research degree, provide a living stipend to help with everyday costs, and offer allowances for expenses such as health cover or relocation. The exact mix depends on the university and the specific offer. A strong applicant might receive a combined package that covers both fees and a stipend, effectively a fully funded research degree.

How to apply for RTP funding

Because universities manage RTP, the process runs through each institution's graduate research office. In practice this means you apply for admission to a research degree and, at the same time or shortly after, apply for a scholarship that draws on RTP funds. Deadlines and application steps vary by university, so check the graduate research scholarship pages for each place you are considering. The role of your research proposal and supervisor

For research degrees, two things matter enormously: a strong research proposal and a supportive potential supervisor. Australian universities generally expect research applicants to identify a supervisor whose expertise matches their project before or during the application. A supervisor who is enthusiastic about your project can strengthen your case for competitive RTP funding.

Your research proposal should be specific, feasible, and clearly aligned with the department's strengths. Spend real time refining it, and pair it with a compelling statement about your motivation and fit. Other study in Australia scholarships

Beyond the two headline schemes, there are more options worth knowing.

University scholarships

Most Australian universities offer their own international scholarships, ranging from partial tuition discounts to substantial merit awards. These can sometimes be combined with other funding and are often easier to win than national schemes. Check each university's international scholarship page for eligibility and deadlines.

Destination Australia

The Destination Australia program supports domestic and international students studying at regional campuses, offering an annual grant to encourage study outside the big cities. If you are open to a regional university, this can meaningfully offset costs.

Research grants and industry-linked scholarships

Some research students receive top-up scholarships attached to specific projects or industry partnerships. These are usually advertised by individual departments or research centres, so it pays to explore the specific labs and groups in your field.

Building a strong application

Whichever route you choose, the fundamentals of a winning application are similar.

Match your goals to the scheme

Australia Awards reward development impact and leadership, while RTP rewards research quality and potential. Frame your application around what the specific scheme values rather than a generic pitch.

Secure admission and identify a supervisor early

Most awards require a course offer, and research awards effectively require a supervisor. Start these conversations months before deadlines so everything lines up in time.

Polish your written materials

Your research proposal, personal statement, and references are where decisions are made. Draft early, seek feedback, and revise. Precise, evidence-backed writing beats vague ambition every time.

Track deadlines carefully

Because RTP runs through each university and Australia Awards run on country-specific timelines, deadlines vary widely. Build a calendar for every scheme and university you are targeting. Eligibility and the return-home commitment

The Australia Awards deserve a closer look at eligibility, because the criteria are specific. Each participating country has its own profile, sometimes prioritising particular fields of study that align with development priorities such as health, education, governance, or infrastructure. Applicants usually must meet minimum age and academic requirements, hold citizenship of an eligible country, and not hold citizenship or permanent residency of Australia or New Zealand.

A defining feature is the requirement to return home for a set period after completing your studies, typically at least two years, before you can apply to live in Australia again. This reflects the development purpose of the program: the goal is for you to bring your new skills back to your community. If your plans involve staying in Australia long term, the Australia Awards may not be the right fit, and a university or research scholarship could suit you better.

University scholarships in more detail

Australia's leading universities compete for strong international students, and many publish generous international scholarships each year. These range from partial tuition reductions applied automatically based on your grades to substantial named awards that require a separate application.

Because amounts, deadlines, and criteria change annually, treat each university's international scholarship page as your primary source. Shortlist a few institutions where your profile is competitive, and apply for every award you are eligible for rather than assuming you will not win. Combining a partial university scholarship with savings or part-time work is a realistic path for many students who do not secure a full government award.

Costs, work rights, and life as a student

Even with a scholarship, it helps to understand the wider cost picture. Living costs differ across Australian cities, with the biggest metros generally more expensive than regional centres, which is part of the appeal of the Destination Australia grants. International students on a student visa can usually work a capped number of hours, which can supplement a partial award.

Factor in health cover, which for many students is arranged through Overseas Student Health Cover, along with visa costs and setup expenses. A clear budget helps you compare the real value of different scholarships rather than just their headline figures.

The RTP stipend and how offers vary

It is worth stressing that RTP funding is not a single fixed award. Universities receive an allocation and design their own scholarship packages around it, so two offers can look quite different. One might waive tuition only, another might add a living stipend, and top offers may include both plus allowances.

Stipend rates are reviewed periodically, and the duration of support is usually tied to the expected length of your degree, often a few years for a PhD with the possibility of a short extension in some circumstances. Always read the specific terms of any RTP-backed offer, including what happens if your research takes longer than planned.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Confusing the Australia Awards, which are development scholarships, with the university-managed RTP.
  • Applying for a research scholarship without first securing a supervisor's support.
  • Overlooking the return-home requirement attached to the Australia Awards.
  • Assuming RTP is a single national award rather than a university-managed package that varies by institution.
  • Missing university-specific deadlines, which differ from the national Australia Awards timeline.

Australia Awards selection and interviews

Selection for the Australia Awards is thorough and looks well beyond grades. Panels assess your academic background, your professional and personal qualities, and, crucially, how your proposed study connects to development outcomes in your home country. Shortlisted applicants are often interviewed, sometimes with a written or aptitude component.

Prepare by articulating a clear story: the problem you care about, how your chosen course equips you to address it, and what you will do when you return home. Concrete examples of leadership and community contribution strengthen your case, as does a realistic and specific plan rather than broad ambition.

Preparing your research proposal for RTP

For research applicants, the proposal is where you win or lose competitive funding. A strong proposal states a clear research question, explains why it matters, outlines a feasible methodology, and shows how it fits the expertise of your chosen department and supervisor.

Avoid trying to sound impressive with an overly broad topic. Assessors value focus and feasibility, evidence that you understand the field, and a project that can realistically be completed within the degree timeframe. Work closely with your prospective supervisor while drafting, since their feedback both improves the proposal and signals their support for your candidacy.

Short-term fellowships and other pathways

The Australia Awards family also includes short-term fellowships aimed at professional and skills development rather than full degrees. These are usually organised through Australian institutions in partnership with organisations in eligible countries, and they can be a route to Australian expertise for mid-career professionals who cannot commit to a full degree abroad. If a long program is not right for your circumstances, exploring these shorter options can still open doors.

Choosing the right university and course

Australia has many strong universities, and your choice shapes both your funding and your experience. For research students, the most important factor is often the presence of a supervisor and a research group aligned with your interests, since that relationship drives access to RTP funding. For coursework students, look closely at each university's international scholarship offerings and the cost of living in its city.

Regional universities can be particularly worth considering, both for programs like Destination Australia and for generally lower living costs. Weigh scholarship value, program quality, location, and the strength of the department in your field together, rather than chasing rankings alone. A well-matched program where you are a strong candidate often beats a more famous name where funding is out of reach.

Final thoughts

The strongest study in Australia scholarships fall into two clear camps. If you are from an eligible partner country and driven by development impact, the Australia Awards offer a comprehensive, fully funded package. If you are heading into research, the Research Training Program, accessed through individual universities and backed by a good supervisor, can fund your master's or PhD. Add university and regional scholarships to the mix, apply early, and Australia becomes a realistic destination without the daunting price tag.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between Australia Awards and the RTP?+

Australia Awards are development scholarships for students from eligible partner countries, covering full costs. The Research Training Program funds research master's and PhD students through universities, typically covering tuition and often a living stipend.

How do I apply for Research Training Program funding?+

RTP funding is distributed by universities, not a central body. You apply for admission to a research degree and then for a scholarship at that university's graduate research office, so deadlines and steps vary by institution.

Do Australia Awards cover living costs?+

Yes. Australia Awards Scholarships are comprehensive, covering tuition, return airfare, an establishment allowance, a contribution to living expenses, health cover, and introductory academic support.

Do I need a supervisor to get a research scholarship in Australia?+

For a research master's or PhD, yes in most cases. Australian universities generally expect you to identify a supervisor whose expertise matches your project, and their support strengthens your case for competitive RTP funding.

Are there scholarships for studying at regional Australian universities?+

Yes. The Destination Australia program offers annual grants to students at regional campuses, and many regional universities run their own international awards, which can significantly reduce your costs.
